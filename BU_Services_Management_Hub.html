Weekly Flow

How the whole team moves together — Sunday to Sunday
The automated backbone: Every Sunday at 7PM ET the Power Automate flow queries Salesforce and writes results to actuals.json on SharePoint. Every Monday at 7AM ET the digest email goes to the commercial leadership group. The Command Centre reads actuals.json live on every page load. Everything else in this rhythm feeds or reads from that backbone.

People Tools

Sprint board  ·  1:1 tracker  ·  Action items
ℹ How to use Sprint Board

Track what each person on the team is focused on this sprint. One item per person — adding a new item for someone replaces their previous one.

  • + Add Sprint Item — pick the person, describe their current focus in plain language, set status and optionally an initiative tag and review date.
  • Status — update to At Risk or Blocked the moment you learn of an issue. Use the filter buttons to quickly surface anyone not On Track.
  • Review date — use the next Commercial or Ops meeting date. Overdue items highlight automatically.
  • Personal use — this data lives in your browser only. Nobody else sees it.
ℹ How to use the 1:1 Tracker

This is Jon's personal log — not a shared team tool. It tracks Jon's own 1:1 conversations with each person on the commercial and ops team. The rest of the team does not see this.

  • Logging a 1:1 — click any person's card to open the log form. Enter the date, schedule the next one, rate engagement, and add a one-line observation to carry forward.
  • One record per person — each save replaces the previous record. The tracker shows the most recent conversation only, not a full history.
  • Engagement level — use 🔴 Low to flag anyone showing early R-03 (retention) signals. Low-engagement cards are badged in red.
  • Overdue — cards badge as Overdue when the next scheduled date has passed, and This Week when it's within 7 days.
  • Not logged — anyone with no 1:1 record yet shows a grey "Not logged" badge — useful to spot gaps when onboarding new team members.
ℹ How to use Action Items

A rolling list of actions from Commercial Meeting, Ops Meeting, Gate Reviews, and Financial Reviews — in one place, not lost in email.

  • + Add Action — describe the action, assign an owner, tag the source meeting, and set a due date.
  • Checking off — click the checkbox to mark an item done. It fades and strikes through. Switch to the Done filter to review what's been completed.
  • Overdue count — shown in red beside the filter tabs. Anything past its due date highlights automatically.
  • Source tags — colour-coded by meeting type so you can see at a glance which meeting generated the most open actions.
  • Personal use — this is Jon's list only. It lives in the browser and is not shared.
