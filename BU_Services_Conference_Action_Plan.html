What This Portal Is

This is the visual review and approval layer for the BU Services 2026 conference programme. It works alongside two Excel files — the Action Tracker and the Budget Model — which you can download using the buttons at the top of the page.

Use this portal to browse all 19 conferences, understand their strategic value, review the action checklists, and make your approval decisions. The Excel files are where the team tracks execution day to day.

Your approval decisions are saved automatically to your browser and persist between sessions — close the tab, come back tomorrow, your decisions are still there.

⭐ Jon's Approval Workflow — Step by Step
  1. Review the Approval Summary Panel
    Scroll up past this section — the Approval Summary Panel is directly below the header. It shows your running count of Approved / On Hold / Rejected / Pending conferences and four live budget totals. This is your scorecard as you work through the list. All zeros until you start deciding.
  2. Check the immediate actions first (Section 01)
    Before diving into individual cards, read Section 01 — What Needs to Happen First. Five blocking actions need to happen immediately regardless of approvals — PPIM travel, NDTMA travel, IPEIA bookings, Mining Expo waitlist, and the API Summit consolidation call with Ekoscan.
  3. Use the Year Calendar for the big picture (Section 02)
    The clickable timeline in Section 02 shows all 19 conferences across 12 months colour-coded by priority. Click any event to jump directly to its card. Use this to get a sense of the full year before approving individual events — check for scheduling conflicts and back-to-back trips.
  4. Review and decide on each conference card (Section 03)
    Each card shows: conference name and dates, priority rating, estimated cost, initiative alignment, strategic score, key attendees, and the full action checklist. At the bottom of every card is the decision bar with three buttons:

    ✓ Approve — card turns green. Budget counted as committed.
    ⏸ Hold — amber border. Needs more information before deciding. Add a note in the text field explaining what you need (e.g. "Confirm waitlist status first").
    ✗ Reject — card dims to 50% opacity with red border. Budget removed from committed total.

    Click the same button again to deselect and return to Pending. Add an optional note to any decision — notes are saved alongside the approval and appear in the export summary.
  5. Use the filter buttons to work through by priority
    Above the conference cards are filter buttons. Click High Priority to see only the 10 must-attend events first — approve those, then work through Medium-High, then Medium. The Low conference (Halifax) is walkin-only at minimal cost and can likely be approved immediately.
  6. Export your decisions
    When you have reviewed all conferences (or a meaningful batch), click 📤 Export Approvals in the summary panel. A modal appears with a formatted text summary of all four lists — Approved, On Hold, Rejected, Pending — with conference names, priorities, costs, and your notes. Click Copy to Clipboard.
  7. Share with the team and update the Excel
    Paste the exported summary into an email or Teams message to Shanny Tremblay and the commercial leads. Then open the Excel Action Tracker → Year Calendar tab → Column M and update each conference's Approval Decision dropdown to match. The budget totals and action tracker approval column update automatically. Rejected conferences show in red on every action item — Shanny can filter and cancel bookings in one pass.
📏 Key Rules — Apply to Every Conference
🔴
48-Hour Salesforce Rule — Non-Negotiable
Every lead from every conference must be in Salesforce within 48 hours of returning home. Tag BU_Initiative__c on every opportunity. This feeds the Sunday Power Automate flow → actuals.json → Command Centre. If it's not in Salesforce, it doesn't exist commercially. No exceptions, no delays.
💰
Personnel Cost Is Your Biggest Hidden Expense
At $180K salary ÷ 235 working days = $766/person/day in unbillable opportunity cost. A 6-person team at IPEIA (3 days + 1.5 travel days) = $28,000 in personnel cost before a single flight is booked. When deciding headcount for each conference, multiply (people × days × $766) and weigh it against expected commercial output.
📅
Quarterly Budget vs Actual Reviews
Jon, Mathieu (EVP), and Jeremy Irwin (Dir. Finance, EIG NA) review actual conference spend against the Budget Model four times per year: Q1: Apr 15 · Q2: Jul 15 · Q3: Oct 15 · Q4: Jan 15, 2027. Shanny submits all receipts to Jeremy before each review date. Update the Actual Cost column (J) in the Action Tracker as invoices arrive.
📦
Book Logistics Early — Especially Europe
Flights from YQB to European shows (ECNDT Verona, Cigre Paris, COFREND Lyon) should be booked 5–6 months in advance to avoid price spikes. ECNDT Verona hotel fills up fast — book March for a June conference. For US shows, 2–3 months is usually sufficient. Shanny tracks all booking deadlines in the Action Tracker.
🤝
Coordinate With Ekoscan BU Before Any Duplicate Bookings
The API Inspection Summit appears on both the Nucleom and Ekoscan conference lists. Before Shanny books booth space or registration for any show where both teams may attend, confirm with Jon and the Ekoscan BU contact first. Combining one booth saves ~$12,000 per show and presents a unified EIG presence to clients.
