01 Your Role in Pipeline DA hide ▴
Delivery operations for BU Services' $30M Pipeline DA initiative
Pipeline DA is a $30M, 5-year initiative — Western Canada-first then continental. Rick Carlton leads the programme commercially and technically at BU Services level. You own the operational delivery — crew management, job execution, quality, and on-time performance.

Rick Carlton and Emmanuel Aggrey jointly own the new Pipeline DA commercial relationships. Your job is delivery: execute the work to a standard that makes those relationships sticky and builds the Western references Rick and Emmanuel need for the continental expansion.

Rule: If a client operator raises a new scope or opportunity with your field crews — flag it to both Rick and Emmanuel that same day. Do not quote or commit to scope without Rick's involvement.
02 Operational Responsibilities hide ▴
Pipeline inspection and DA delivery: Manage the PI group's dedicated technician pool for all active Pipeline DA contracts. These technicians work exclusively in pipeline integrity — not drawn from or shared with Nortech's core legacy business. Ensure crew are trained, qualified, and current on Nortech's DA methodology and toolset. Escalate to Jason Borza (Nortech Ops Director) when you need cross-Nortech support or resource decisions above your level.
Field intelligence — 3X and new opportunities: Your crew are in the field on pipeline assets. When they find AMBER or RED conditions — corrosion, anomalies, structural issues — that is a 3X Composite Repair opportunity.

Same-day flagging protocol: Any AMBER/RED finding goes to Emmanuel Aggrey (Commercial Director West) the same day it is identified. Not the next weekly meeting. Your crew see things in the field that the commercial team never will. Train them to report.
