Executive Summary
Why Nucleom wins in powerline inspection

Canada operates one of the world’s most extensive high-voltage transmission networks. Aging infrastructure, growing renewable energy connections, and grid uprating programs are creating unprecedented demand for advanced conductor and splice inspection — work that cannot be deferred and cannot be done visually. Nucleom holds the only Canadian-owned suite of three proprietary tools for this market, leading the Powerline Inspection initiative across the BU Services platform with Nortech providing Western Canada (Alberta) commercial support.

Unique IP

LineCore (ECA), LineOhm (resistance), LineRay (X-ray). Three tools — no competitor offers this complete suite on live lines to 735kV.

Proven Platform

Nucleom already holds OPG contracts and multi-sector NDT credibility. Powerline extends the same trusted brand into a safety-critical vertical.

Market Timing

Canadian utilities face multi-decade re-investment cycles. Hydro-Québec, BC Hydro, AltaLink, and Hydro One are actively procuring condition-based inspection services.

Technology Portfolio
Three tools. One complete transmission line inspection solution.

Nucleom’s powerline inspection capability rests on three purpose-built instruments, each addressing a distinct failure mode and complementary to the others.

LineCore — Conductor Zinc Layer Assessment (ECA)

What It Does

An Eddy Current Array probe that detects progressive degradation of the protective zinc layer on ACSR conductors — the key early-warning indicator of corrosion-driven conductor failure. Operates on energized lines and moves autonomously.

Conductor typeACSR
Conductor diameter15 mm to 43.18 mm
Inspection speedUp to 1.0 m/s
Line voltageEnergized to 735 kV
Conductor temperatureTested to 100°C
Ambient temperature5°C to 30°C
DeploymentLineDrone, pole-mounted robot, carrier
Why Clients Choose LineCore
  • Early DetectionIdentifies zinc degradation before corrosion reaches the steel core — planned maintenance vs. emergency replacement.
  • No Line ShutdownInspects at up to 735 kV while energized. Zero production loss for the utility.
  • Motorized AutonomousDouble-wheel drive enables continuous travel; covers long spans without lineman involvement.
  • Uprating ValidationConfirms conductor condition for Dynamic Line Rating (DLR) feasibility — a growing utility requirement.

LineOhm — Splice Sleeve Electrical Resistance Assessment

What It Does

Measures the micro-ohm electrical resistance of compression splice sleeves on energized lines. Elevated resistance is the leading indicator of overheating and splice failure — invisible to visual or thermal inspection. Based on a Hydro-Québec patent.

Splice diameter15 mm to 70 mm
Conductor diameter15 mm to 45 mm
Operating current2.5 A to 3,000 A
Adjustable length19 cm to 33 cm
Line voltageEnergized to 735 kV
Weight<500 g (aluminum housing)
Range of motion~360° (ball-and-socket joint)
Hydro-Québec Patented Technology
  • No Surface PrepPatented contact plate system eliminates conductor cleaning, cutting field time significantly.
  • Low-Current UseOperates at minimum 2.5 A — usable on adjacent de-energized circuits without an external source.
  • Full 360° FreedomBall-and-socket joint allows use in constrained tower geometry from a pole or platform.
  • Paired with LineRayComplete mechanical + electrical splice assessment in one mobilization.

LineRay — Internal X-Ray Inspection of Powerline Components

✓
The Invisible Made VisibleVisual and thermal inspections cannot see inside metal. LineRay uses X-ray imaging to detect hidden defects inside critical overhead powerline components — without dismantling, without outages.

Components Inspected

  • Compression sleeves (splices)
  • Connectors and terminals
  • Bolted joints
  • Dead ends and dampers
  • Ground wire connections

Hidden Defects Detected by X-Ray

  • Broken conductor strands under compression sleeves
  • Voids, cracks, or poor crimps inside joints
  • Water or contamination causing internal corrosion

Market Opportunity
Canadian utilities face a multi-decade re-investment cycle. The window is now.

Canada operates over 160,000 km of high-voltage transmission lines, much of it built in the 1960s–1980s and now approaching or exceeding design service life. Utilities face mounting pressure from regulators, insurers, and reliability standards bodies to document conductor condition systematically.

Key Market Drivers
Grid DecarbonizationRenewable integration requires expanded transmission and systematic conductor condition monitoring to ensure existing lines can handle increased loads safely.
Aging InfrastructureUtilities face NERC/FERC reliability mandates with conductor fleets well beyond design life. Reactive maintenance is no longer acceptable to regulators.
Uprating DemandDLR uprating programs require a conductor condition baseline. LineCore is the only live-line ECA tool for this assessment in Canada.
Insurance & Risk ManagementInsurers and regulators require documented condition assessments before lines are operated above nameplate capacity or approved for life extension.

Go-To-Market Strategy
From Hydro-Québec reference to national utility presence — 2026 to 2030.

Nucleom’s powerline commercial strategy follows a three-phase build — each phase funded by revenue generated in the prior phase, with no requirement for speculative investment.

Phase 1 — 2026

Anchor & Validate

  1. Execute Hydro-Québec reference campaign with LineCore + LineOhm on TransÉnergie lines
  2. Publish case study: zinc degradation + splice resistance results
  3. Initiate Hydro One and BC Hydro vendor qualification (Vinoth Tharma — East)
  4. Develop LineRay service offering: safety protocols, radiation licensing, SOP

Phase 2 — 2027

Scale Nationally

  1. Convert Hydro One and BC Hydro to multi-year service agreements
  2. Launch bundled 3-tool inspection programs in a single mobilization
  3. Enter Alberta via AltaLink / ATCO through Nortech AB relationships (Max Aggrey)
  4. Submit to utility vendor prequalification portals (MERX, Hydro One, BC Hydro)

Phase 3 — 2028–2030

Recurring Platform

  1. Annual conductor audit contracts with 5+ utilities across Canada
  2. US expansion via EIG France / Eurazeo network — NERC-regulated IOUs (2029)
  3. IP licensing and instrument rental as secondary revenue streams
  4. Integrate with EIG SG NDT EMMA software for unified data reporting
