Rules of Engagement

how we operate commercially — non-negotiable standards
Always do
Lead with results, not technology. Open every new account conversation with a customer outcome — "Barrick has been running this for two years and found three high-severity gear defects before they became failures." Not: "We have an ECA instrument."

Quote the integrated program. Every mining proposal includes both ECA gear inspection and robotic UT shell survey. Every proposal with AMBER/RED findings includes a 3X composite repair option. One invoice, one mobilisation, maximum revenue per visit.

Confirm ops capacity before quoting a field date. Call Eric Rodrigue (East) or Jason Borza (West) before putting a mobilisation date in a proposal. A date we cannot staff is worse than no date.

Log everything in Salesforce the same day. Call with a prospect: log it. Proposal issued: log it as an opportunity. Deal closed: update to Closed Won immediately.
Never do
Do not promise powerline delivery dates before EIG instrument model is confirmed. If a utility asks when you can start, say "Q3 2026 is our target window — we are confirming instrument availability now." Never commit a date that depends on a decision that has not been made.

Do not discount below the rate card without Olivier Marcotte's approval. Mining inspection is a specialist service with pricing power. Discount only on program volume (3+ mills) and only at rates explicitly in the rate card. Unilateral discounting trains clients to push back every time.

Do not skip the vendor qualification step. Utility clients (Hydro One, BC Hydro) require formal vendor registration before they can issue a purchase order. Submit documents the day the pilot proposal is issued — never wait for a "yes" first.

Do not let a deal stall silently. If a proposal has been out for more than 14 days without a response, call the contact directly. A deal sitting in "Proposal Issued" in Salesforce with no activity is a deal you are losing.
Escalation: when to bring in Jon or Olivier
Bring in Olivier Marcotte when: a deal exceeds $500K; a client raises a competitive threat from Applus RTD, Eddyfi/Baker Hughes, or Olympus IMS; a proposal has been rejected and you cannot determine why; OPG sends any signal about the MSA process outside of a scheduled meeting.

Bring in Jon Eustace when: a deal exceeds $2M; a client requests an executive-level meeting; a contract term requires approval above your authority level; you are considering a strategic partnership or subcontracting arrangement not already in the plan.
Early warning signals — flag these at Monday's meeting immediately
Mining R-05: Fewer than 3 new accounts signed by end of Q2. Vinoth/Max pipeline generating fewer than 10 proposals combined. Gold price correction below US$3,000/oz.

Powerline R-06: EIG has not confirmed instrument terms by May 15. HQ 2026 scope not confirmed. Hydro One pilot rejected with no alternative secured.

Pipeline R-02: Pembina vendor approval stalled past May 23 (Rick Carlton's deal — but Max needs to be ready to activate Alberta independent operators as backup).

Nuclear R-01: OPG signals anything unusual about the MSA process. Applus RTD news (acquisition, recapitalisation, new hire in nuclear division).
