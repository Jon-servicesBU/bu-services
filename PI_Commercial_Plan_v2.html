Executive Summary
DA market context, revenue scenarios, and strategic objectives

Canadian pipeline operators face a growing and non-deferrable integrity management challenge. CSA Z662 and the Canada Energy Regulator (CER) mandate systematic direct assessment programs for all transmission and distribution pipelines where inline inspection pigging is not feasible or not elected. The DA market represents a multi-hundred-million dollar annual spend across Canada, driven by aging infrastructure, increasing regulatory scrutiny, and the financial consequences of pipeline incidents.

The Opportunity
Market size, regulatory drivers, and tailwinds

Canada operates one of the most extensive pipeline networks in the world, with over 840,000 km of pipelines carrying natural gas, crude oil, NGL, and refined products. For pipelines where ILI is not conducted, CSA Z662 Clause 10 mandates a systematic DA process. DA programs are not optional, not deferrable, and cannot be avoided by any regulated operator.

Market Size

  • Canada has 73,000+ km of CER-regulated transmission pipelines, plus 770,000+ km of provincially regulated distribution and gathering systems
  • Conservative estimate: 5,000+ direct assessment digs per year across Canadian transmission lines
  • Average dig program value (pre-survey + excavation + PAUT + FFS): CAD $12,000 to $35,000 per dig
  • Total Canadian DA dig market estimated at CAD $200M to $400M+ annually
  • BC and Alberta represent the largest concentration of midstream infrastructure outside Ontario, with Pembina, Tidewater, TC Energy, and ATCO among the largest spenders

Regulatory Drivers

  • CSA Z662: mandatory integrity management provisions including DA program requirements for unpigged lines
  • CER OPR-99: operators must file and maintain integrity management programs with CER oversight and audit authority
  • CEPA integrity management protocols: industry standard above regulatory minimum
  • Increased CER audit activity since 2016 has intensified operator urgency around defensible, documented DA programs
  • Provincial regulators (AER in Alberta, BCOGC in BC) maintain parallel requirements, expanding the addressable market beyond CER jurisdiction

Service Offering
ECDA, ICDA, SCCDA, ILI verification, PAUT/UT, FFS assessment

The BU - Services delivers pipeline integrity direct assessment as a fully integrated offering. Rather than treating ECDA, ICDA, SCCDA, and ILI verification as separate services, we mobilize a single program capable of addressing all four assessment types concurrently — reducing client coordination burden and total program cost. PAUT/UT corrosion mapping is embedded in every dig program. All services are delivered without pigging and meet CSA Z662 and CER documentation standards.

External Corrosion Direct Assessment
Complete 4-step ECDA per NACE SP0502 and CSA Z662: pre-assessment, indirect examination (CIS, ACVG, DCVG, PCM), direct examination at excavations, post-assessment with CSA Z662-compliant documentation. SCCDA per NACE SP0204 and CSA Z662 Annex N: terrain analysis, CP shielding evaluation, coating assessment, PAUT crack detection at excavations. Findings are reported with SCC observations and PAUT data; SCC fitness-for-service decisions rest with the client’s engineering team.

The Core Service — ILI Tool Verification Programs
ILI verification is the primary commercial service. The core package includes: pre-dig review, terrain analysis, coating and deposit assessment, ILI anomaly verification, magnetic particle inspection (MPI), phased array ultrasonic testing (PAUT), time-of-flight diffraction (TOFD), ultrasonic testing (UT), corrosion assessment, geometric defect assessment, defect removal support, repair inspection, and recoat documentation. On-site preliminary reporting is delivered at the completion of each assessment. Additional capabilities: CP readings, soil resistivity testing, location verification, girth weld and seam inspection, and comprehensive final reporting with full photographic documentation.

Full-coverage encoded PAUT scans at excavations. C-scan thickness maps colour-coded vs. nominal specification. Crack detection capability (TOFD/tandem). SG NDT instrument technology advantage within the Ekoscan group. Typically 1–2 days per excavation.

ASME B31G, Modified B31G, CSA Z662 Annex O Level 1 and Level 2 calculations. Delivered as completed calculations and field-observed findings to support the client’s engineering team.
