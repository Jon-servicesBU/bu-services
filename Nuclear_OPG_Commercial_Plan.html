The Commercial Thesis
Nucleom's inherited OPG relationship + new build and refurbishment credentials = 2027 MSA expansion

Nucleom's nuclear commercial strategy is built on a foundation that Nucleom (formerly Arcanite Inc., amalgamated January 1, 2026) spent five years constructing — a direct OPG Master Services Agreement covering ten work packages across steam generators, feeders, turbines, balance of plant, and quality control at Pickering and Darlington. That MSA ran from April 2020 to December 2024, with two optional one-year extensions taking it to end of 2026. The 2027 renewal is not a routine contract extension. It is a strategic opportunity to fundamentally expand the scope and value of Nucleom's OPG relationship.

The timing has never been better. OPG is simultaneously managing the world's largest active CANDU refurbishment program at Pickering, the construction of North America's first commercial grid-scale SMR at Darlington, and the ongoing maintenance of its active operating fleet. The Outage Revenue Model — Why Maintenance Shutdowns Are the Prize

Nuclear power plants are shut down on a planned schedule for maintenance, inspection, and refuelling. These "planned outages" (POs) are intense, time-compressed programs — typically 3 to 6 weeks — during which large volumes of inspection and maintenance work are completed simultaneously under significant cost and schedule pressure. OPG relies heavily on augmented staff from qualified contractors to supplement its own workforce during outages.

Proposed 2027 Rates
Updated from 2020 baseline — includes LOA supplement and new mob/demob structure

The 2020 Arcanite MSA rates were set at a level that did not adequately cover all-in technician deployment costs. Five years of partial escalation (3.6–5.1%/yr) has compounded the problem — 2024/2025 rates remain below the level required to cover LOA costs, mobilisation and demobilisation expenses, and provide Nucleom with a sustainable margin on outage work. The 2027 MSA renewal is the opportunity to reset rates to a defensible, commercially viable level.

Escalation Basis

2 years at 6.5%/yr from 2024/2025 rates. Nuclear NDT labour is in high demand with both the Darlington SMR and Pickering Refurbishment running simultaneously — standard CPI escalation understates true market movement. 6.5% is defensible and reflects actual market conditions in the Ontario nuclear supply chain.

LOA Supplement

+$10/hr embedded in Mon-Fri day shift rate. OPG's existing daily rate ($120/day for Pickering and Darlington) covers accommodation but leaves a gap. Actual technician LOA all-in cost is approximately $180–200/day. The $60–80/day shortfall (~$7.50–10/hr) must be embedded in the hourly rate. This is a pass-through cost recovery, not additional margin.

Mob/Demob — New Line Item

$1,200 per technician per mobilisation, $1,200 per demobilisation. The 2020 MSA had zero mob/demob recovery. Economy airfare + ground transport to Pickering or Darlington typically costs $800–1,400 per round trip from Quebec City or elsewhere in Canada. $1,200 each way is a fair, defensible rate that OPG can accept as genuine cost recovery.

Mob/Demob — Proposed New Schedule
!
This is a Material Negotiation Point — The 2020 MSA contained no mob/demob provisions. Every technician deployment to Pickering or Darlington from Quebec City costs Nucleom approximately $800–1,400 in airfare alone, plus ground transport. On a 12-person outage crew, Nucleom absorbs $10,000–$17,000 in unrecovered mob costs per outage — and again on demob. Across 5–8 outages per year, this is a six-figure annual cost that the business currently cannot recover. The 2027 MSA must explicitly address this.

Proposed Mob Fee

$1,200 per technician per mobilisation.

Covers: economy class airfare from home base to site (or equivalent ground transport), ground transportation to Pickering / Darlington from nearest airport, and first-night accommodation if arrival day precedes first shift.

Invoiced per technician named on each Contract Order / Crew Schedule at time of outage commencement.

Proposed Demob Fee

$1,200 per technician per demobilisation.

Covers: ground transport from site to airport, economy class return airfare to home base, and any incidentals associated with end-of-assignment departure.

Invoiced per technician named on each Contract Order at time of outage completion or technician release, consistent with the Crew Schedule demobilisation date.

Aecon Capital Programs — Strategic Context

Nucleom holds active NDT subcontracts through Aecon on both the Darlington BWRX-300 SMR and the Pickering Units 5–8 Refurbishment. These programs are described in detail in Section 5. Their revenues are significant — estimated at $11.3M and $18.7M respectively over the plan period — but are accounted for separately and are not included in BU Services Nuclear initiative plan totals. They are presented here as strategic context because they are the single most powerful credential Nucleom can bring to the 2027 MSA tender negotiation.

Darlington SMR — Aecon Subcontract Not in plan totals
Full NDT scope via Aecon-Kiewit Nuclear Partners. North America's first commercial grid-scale SMR. ~$11.3M Nucleom NDT revenue 2026–2030. Strongest possible construction NDT credential for the MSA renewal conversation at the same Darlington site.
Pickering Refurb — Aecon Subcontract Not in plan totals
NDT subcontract via Aecon / AtkinsRéalis (CanAtom JV). ~$18.7M Nucleom NDT revenue over the program. NEAR RT feeder system, PAUT/UT, 48 steam generator replacements. Directly reinforces Nucleom's MSA feeder inspection and SG analysis credentials.

Reading the Scenarios — Case 1 (25%) is the plan basis: T.R.U.S.T.I.E. + ET remote analysis — the packages Nucleom already holds as incumbent. This is the conservative, bankable foundation. Cases 2–4 represent the commercial upside as Nucleom displaces Intech (unable to fully supply) and Applus RTD (financially distressed). The QCCC union dynamic means the technicians required for expanded scope come with the contracts — not a hiring constraint. OPG has explicitly invited Nucleom to bid at full scope, making Cases 2–4 realistic targets in the 2028–2030 period.
