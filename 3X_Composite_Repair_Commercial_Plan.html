Pricing Model
Product + installation pricing by application — additive to existing inspection contract values

Every composite repair is individually engineered. 3X Engineering’s REA finite element analysis software calculates the exact product quantities required for each defect based on pipe diameter, wall thickness, defect dimensions, operating pressure, and temperature. Product cost is then determined directly from those quantities against the 2026 3X North America price list. BU Services adds its distribution margin on top of the product cost, plus installation labour based on crew size and duration.

●
How Every Repair is Engineered and Priced

Step 1 — Defect characterisation: BU Services inspection delivers the key inputs — remaining wall thickness (from PAUT/UT), defect type and geometry, pipe or vessel outside diameter, design pressure (MAOP), and operating temperature. These are the primary variables that drive the repair design.

Step 2 — REA FEA calculation: 3X’s REA finite element analysis software takes those inputs and calculates the number of composite wrap layers required to restore the component to full design pressure and safety factor. A deeper defect, higher MAOP, or more severe temperature condition requires more wrap layers. REA outputs the required number of Kevlar® tape layers and the resin system reference (e.g. R3X55 for standard temperature, R3X95 for elevated, R3X150+ for high-temperature or through-wall).

Step 3 — Quantity derivation: The number of wrap layers × pipe circumference × defect overlap length = total tape length consumed. Tape length determines the number of rolls of the specified width. Resin kit consumption is calculated from the tape surface area to be impregnated. All quantities flow directly from the REA output — no estimating.

Step 4 — Product cost: REA-calculated quantities × 2026 3X North America CAD price list + BU Services distribution margin = product cost.

Step 5 — Installation labour: 4-tech crew × $800/day/tech = $3,200/day crew rate. Crew days estimated from repair scope and layer count. More layers = more wrapping time. Complex or first-of-type repairs include a 3X lead tech at initial stage.

Step 6 — Client quote: Product cost + installation labour + engineering/documentation = total repair price. Because REA drives the quantities, the quote is fully defensible and traceable to the inspection finding.

Canadian Regulatory Qualification — ABSA Pathway & Status

ABSA (Alberta Boilers Safety Association) governs the registration and use of engineered composite repair systems (ECS) on pressure equipment in Alberta. Approval by ABSA is recognized nationally — once ABSA approvals are in place, TSASK, TSBC, TSSA, and RBQ will follow. This is the single most important commercial enabler for the Canadian pressure equipment market. BU Services is actively progressing through this process.

ABSA Qualification Process — Where We Stand (April 2026)
Step 1 — Company QMS Registration (Form AB-29): Applied as Arcanite with the ECS Manual packaged. Accepted by ABSA. Now pausing to re-submit under Nortech (existing ABSA-approved QMS) and Nucleom (QMS being drafted from the Nortech program). Nucleom will require an in-person ABSA audit to achieve certification. ✓ Initial acceptance achieved
Step 2 — QMS Certification + ISI Application: Once QMS is certified via application and audit, the ECS manual is re-introduced under the certified QMS. Simultaneously, apply for ISI (in-service inspection) — permits visual inspection under API 510/570.
Step 3 — Procedure Applications: Submit individual composite repair procedures to ABSA. 3X Engineering has streamlined the procedures into 3 consolidated submissions to minimize audit scope while maximising coverage: Type A Internal Defect; Type A External Defect; and Type B Internal/External combined. This is a significant efficiency gain over submitting per-product.
Step 4 — Competency Demonstration: Demonstrate installer competency across all applicable piping components referenced in the procedures: straight pipe, elbows, tees, and small attachment fittings.
Step 5 — Lead Installer Qualification (AB-539): ABSA AB-539 governs engineered composite systems. The ECS manual was written precisely to the AB-539 standard, using ABSA's own language throughout. Key constraint: lead installers must have 3 years minimum field experience; assistants require 6 months. BU Services is assessing which technicians qualify under this threshold.
Step 6 — Per-Repair ABSA Applications: Once all of the above are satisfied, each individual repair in Western Canada still requires its own ABSA application and approval before installation can proceed. There is no blanket approval. Every single repair is applied for individually. ABSA parameters: maximum 500 psi, maximum 250°C, piping only (pressure vessels excluded under current ABSA framework). Note: 3X products are rated well beyond these limits; the ABSA restrictions are regulatory, not technical.
